# How to Protect Yourself Against Phishing Attacks

Phishing remains one of the most common, effective, and dangerous cyber threats organizations face today. These attacks target the weakest link in every company: **human behavior**. No matter how advanced your technical defenses are, attackers will always try to manipulate employees through social engineering techniques.

![Protecting Against Phishing Attacks](/blog/phishing-photo-1.png "Strategies to protect against phishing attacks")

In this guide, we explain what phishing really is, how attackers operate, and how organizations can build strong defenses. You'll also discover how **Phishy's advanced phishing simulation and training platform** helps companies strengthen their internal security culture.


# What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack technique in which threat actors use email, SMS, voice calls, or social media messages to trick users into revealing sensitive information, such as:

- Passwords  
- Financial details  
- Personal data  
- System access credentials  

Phishing attacks have evolved dramatically in recent years, becoming more realistic, personalized, and harder to detect.


# Common Phishing Techniques

## Email Phishing

The most widespread form of phishing. Attackers send emails that appear to come from trusted companies, colleagues, banks, or internal departments.

## Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks aimed at a specific person or organization. The attacker gathers intelligence about the victim and crafts messages tailored to them—making the attack far more convincing.

## Whaling (Executive-Level Phishing)

Specialized attacks that target CEOs, executives, and other high-level personnel to gain access to sensitive data or trick them into authorizing payments.

Defending against phishing requires a **multi-layered strategy**, not a single tool or solution.

The strongest defense against phishing is *educated employees*.  
Organizations that regularly perform phishing simulations and training programs dramatically reduce risk.

## 2. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even if a password is compromised, MFA provides an additional layer of protection.  
Forms of MFA include:

- SMS verification  
- Mobile app approval  
- Hardware keys  

Every account that supports MFA should have it enabled.


## 3. Advanced Email Filtering

Effective filtering includes:

- Sender verification

- Attachment scanning  
- Behavioral analysis
